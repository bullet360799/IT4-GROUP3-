How to Prepare Noodles

A simple narrated recipe for making tasty noodles at home.

Ingredients

  • 200g dried noodles (egg noodles, ramen, or rice noodles)
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 small onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 medium carrot, julienned
  • 1 cup shredded cabbage or spinach
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce (or to taste)
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce or hoisin (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Spring onions, chopped (for garnish)

Equipment

  • Large pot for boiling
  • Colander or sieve
  • Large frying pan or wok
  • Spatula or tongs

Step-by-step Preparation

  1. Boil the noodles:

    Fill a large pot with water and bring it to a rolling boil. Add a pinch of salt and the dried noodles. Cook according to package instructions until just tender (al dente). This usually takes 3–5 minutes for most dried noodles.

  2. Drain and rinse:

    Drain the noodles in a colander and rinse briefly under warm water to stop the cooking and remove excess starch. Toss with a teaspoon of oil to prevent sticking.

  3. Prepare the vegetables:

    Heat the frying pan or wok over medium-high heat. Add the vegetable oil, then sauté garlic and onion until fragrant and translucent (about 1–2 minutes). Add carrots and cook 2–3 minutes, then add cabbage or spinach and cook until wilted.

  4. Combine and season:

    Add the cooked noodles to the pan. Drizzle soy sauce and oyster/hoisin sauce if using. Toss everything together for 1–2 minutes until the noodles are heated through and evenly coated. Taste and adjust seasoning with salt, pepper, or more soy sauce.

  5. Serve:

    Plate the noodles, garnish with chopped spring onions, and serve immediately.

Tips & Variations

  • Add cooked protein like shredded chicken, tofu, or prawns for a heartier meal.
  • For spicier noodles, add a teaspoon of chili paste or a few dashes of hot sauce.
  • If using rice noodles, soak them in hot water until pliable instead of boiling to avoid overcooking.

Notes

This is a base recipe—feel free to swap vegetables and sauces to match your taste or what you have on hand.
